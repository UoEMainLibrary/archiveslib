Baillie | Florence Jewel | nee Fowler | 1893-1969 | wife of John Baillie

Biographical Information

Occupation, Sphere of Activity

Florence Jewel Fowler was born in 1893, at Erdington, Warwickshire. During the First World War, she was initially a nurse and then volunteered for service with the YMCA in France, where she met John Baillie. He enlisted her to assist with lectures he gave to the soldiers. After the war, in 1919, they married at Leamington Spa.

Jewel took an active interest in John Baillie's work, accompanying when he travelled, sitting on charitable committees, looking after missionary furlough houses and much more. She also wrote a preface to his 'Baptism and Conversion' and a foreward to his 'A Reasoned Faith'.

Relationships

Florence Jewel Baillie was the daughter of Richard Fowler, land agent & surveyor, and his wife Florence Pearse. She was the wife of theologian John Baillie.
