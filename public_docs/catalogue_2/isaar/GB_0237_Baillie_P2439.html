Dusen | Henry Pitney | Van | 1897-1975 | ecumenical leader

Biographical Information

Occupation, Sphere of Activity

Henry Pitney Van Dusen was educated at The University of Princeton, New College, Edinburgh, and at Union Theological Seminary, New York. He returned to Edinburgh as a postgraduate student. His challenge of the literal interpretation of the virgin birth became an obstacle to ordination but this was overcome with the support of the likes of John Foster Dulles.

Teaching initially at various American colleges, he returned to take up a post at Union Theological Seminary in 1926. An international figure, he was prominent in founding the World Council of Churches.

Relationships

Henry Pitney Van Dusen married Elizabeth Coghill Bartholomew, daughter of Scottish cartographer John George Bartholomew

Other Significant Information

Notable publications:

World Christianity: yesterday, today, tomorrow, (1948)

Honours, Qualifications and Appointments

1919: graduated, University of Princeton

1924: B.D., Union Theological Seminary, New York

1932: Ph.D., University of Edinburgh

1936: appointed Roosevelt Professor of Systematic Theology, Union Theological Seminary, New York

1945: appointed President, Union Theological Seminary, New York
